What is Base58 Encoding?

Base58 is a binary-to-text encoding scheme that is primarily used in cryptocurrency applications, such as Bitcoin addresses. It is designed to be more user-friendly than Base64 by excluding characters that can be easily confused, such as 0 (zero) and O (capital o), making it ideal for human-readable applications.
